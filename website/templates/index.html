Parkinson's

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is a debilitating, incurable neurodegenerative disease that affects a significant segment of the population, causing a large societal and financial burden. PD has been a focus of intensive research; however, the causes and biological mechanisms underlying the syndrome are still poorly understood, and the search for a cure continues.

In the hope of shedding light on the roots of Parkinson's Disease, PPMI, an ongoing large clinical study, performs a wide array of investigations on Parkinson's patients and healthy controls, ranging from neurological studies to genetics and medical imaging. The many features examined, and the large number of subjects enrolled, make PPMI data ideally suited to computational exploration.

SPEECH

Over the past week, have you had problems with your speech?

Normal[0]: Not at all (no problems). Slight[1-3]: Speech is soft, slurred or uneven, but it does not cause others to ask to repeat what is said. Mild[4-6]: Speech is sometimes not understood, but not everyday. Moderate[7-9]: Speech is unclear enough that others will ask for clarification every day, although most speech is intelligible. Severe[10]: Most or all speech cannot be understood.

HAND WRITING

Over the past week, have people usually had trouble reading your handwriting?

Normal[0]: Not at all (no problems). Slight[1-3]: Writing is slow, clumsy or uneven, but all words are clear. Mild[4-6]: Some words are unclear and difficult to read. Moderate[7-9]: Many words are unclear and difficult to read. Severe[10]: Most or all words cannot be read.

TREMOR

Over the past week, have you usually had shaking or tremor?

Normal[0]: Not at all (no problems). Slight[1-3]: Shaking or tremor occurs but does not cause problems with any activities. Mild[4-6]: Shaking or tremor causes problems with only a few activities. Moderate[7-9]: Shaking or tremor causes problems with many daily activities. Severe[10]: Shaking or tremor causes problems with most or all activities.
